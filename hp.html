HP Case Study

An in depth review of HP's Smart App and the process of printing from mobile devices, while also user testing their HP Tango Printer that they sent to us and redesigning the HP Smart App.

Role

UI/UX Designer & Researcher

Tasks

UX Research
UX Design
UI Design
User Testing

Tools

Adobe XD

Timeline

Sept - Dec 2019

Background

Our team analyzed and redesigned the HP Smart App, which gives users the ability to connect to their web-based printer and print straight from their phones. We also user tested the HP Tango printer. This was a case study for HP that we conducted in teams in our User Experience Class. Our professor previously worked for HP and was able to get the company to send our class their HP Tango printers that we would set up and use with their HP Smart App.

Our Task

Our task was to evaluate the HP Smart app and assess how it worked with their HP Tango printer. We were also asked to conduct an OOBE (out-of-box experiment) tests and upon initial set up, we began our research and using the app. We were tasked to test the current application and redesign it based off our collected data.

The Results

By understanding the pain points users had while using the printer and the corresponding app. We were able to redesign it based off the feedback we received to the current production app, as well as understand the data being pulled from the printer.

UX Process

Research

Conducted research on existing HP products that were on the market, along with the HP Tango printer and looked into the similarities and differences between them. Sixty participants were then surveyed about their current printer situation and their habits. We then used the data that was collected from those participants to formulate more detailed questions to conduct recorded interviews with four participants.

Personas

Through our research, we identified types of users who are actively looking for solutions to print more sufficiently and effectively for their day-to-day work. 

User Testing

We conducted a OOBE tests, where we observed and recorded seven participants' actions to set up the HP Tango printer. The takeaways from this experiment from the participants was that the design of the printer was clean, with a simple interface.

We found three major pain points:
- Poor Network Connectivity
- Sending documents to printers
- Reviewing the manual to trouble shoot issues

User Journey Map

Above is a visualization of the interactions for the user's process. The stars represent similarities in feedback we received from the users. The Green stars represent the positive reactions, the yellow represent OK reactions, and the red represents negative reactions.

Based off our evaluations from our research, we found that many issues stem from the current HP Smart App and began an app analysis to address those problems. 

App Analysis

We conducted an app analysis on the current state of the app and identified concerns and issues with the HP Smart App.

· It was unclear how to add a printer.
· Unclear what the printer status was.
· It was not obvious what features were currently unavailable for that printer, unless the user tapped on the tile, which displayed a modal letting us know.
· Users can customize tiles they want shown but shouldn't it already set up what is available and what is not automatically? 

· On the home page, when we removed the title beneath the icons, we found that there was some difficulty understanding what the sections represented.
· Print documents button and view & print button on the navigation bar take you to the same place, seems unnecessary. 

· The Drop down menu had a long list of printers to scroll through.
· There was no easy way to type a printer name to search.

· When setting the printer up, we encountered an issue with the next button. Becomes unresponsive and impossible to press, making us unable to complete the set up process.
· When there is no camera access, the bottom bar is still present and is occupying space for no reason.

Wireframe & Paper Prototype

After collecting our data from the surveys and interviews we conducted, we decided to focus our attention mainly on the printing and scanning flows, since those were the most frequently used features amongst our users.

Reflection

Thanks to our professor, our class was able to conduct a study with HP to review and redesign their HP Smart App and work with printers they provided our class. Our team learned a lot from this experience and the types of research methods needed in order to understand the users we were designing for. OOBE tests allowed us to observe users interactions with the product and provided us with feedback necessary to understand what they were looking to accomplish and highlighted what their frustrations were. Journey maps allowed us to visualize the interactions taken by the user, which provided insight from the customer perspective. Many of the steps taken helped us with our analysis and redesign of the app, underlining areas of the app that needed to be fixed based off the data we received during this study. Overall, this was a wonderful learning experience for us and it was great being able to present our findings and ideas to HP.
