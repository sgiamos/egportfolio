WATERLY

Providing our users with simple, on-the-go access to water quality information anywhere in the United States.

This was a project that was completed over a four month period for my thesis studio class. The topic of the project was Climate Change or pollution related and we were asked to come up with an idea using a medium of choice.

Overview

The Problem:
A lot of water pollution stems from human activities. Contaminants in our waters sometimes prevents us from being able to enjoy these bodies of water on hot summer days. Waterly provides our users with simple, on-the-go access to water quality information on their local beaches, lakes, and rivers. As well as any other location they wish to visit across the United States.

The Goal:
We give users the ability to check the waters for information regarding health risks they may face while venturing out in areas with advisories that are disrupting water quality, as well as air quality. Waterly also provides information on lifeguard statuses, parking, restrooms, and food and drinks. In order to prevent water pollution, we have a community page where members of our app can register and join local communities in cleaning up.

Research

I conducted extensive research on water pollution and how it or if it effects our quality. While researching that, I was also looking into websites and applications that currently alert people about water quality at beaches, rivers, and lakes. All while looking into how they relay that information to those applications. I reached out to an environmental scientist that works for the New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection, who verifies data and then releases the data to go to the public facing njbeaches.org website. The environmental scientist stated that, "the website uses a logic scheme to know whether a beach will go into advisory or closure based on the current sample result and the prior sample result. Beaches may also be closed in the system manually by DEP or the local health authority."
