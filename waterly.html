WATERLY

Providing our users with simple, on-the-go access to water quality information anywhere in the United States.

This was a project that was completed over a four month period for my thesis studio class. The topic of the project was Climate Change or pollution related and we were asked to come up with an idea using our design medium of choice.

Background

Water pollution comes from substances that contaminate our waters, which can be harmful to humans and our environment. Most water pollution stems from human activities, which leads our waters to be completely unusable and negatively impacting our society. Contaminants in our waters sometimes prevents us from being able to enjoy these bodies of water on hot summer days.

The Problem

As beach or lake goers, when we come across closures, we often wonder what happened or why? As we search for an answer, we come across several sites with explanations but not ones we can easily understand.
How can we help users understand the information presented to them while staying up to date on water quality information while they're on the go?

The Solution

By providing users with simple, on-the-go access to water quality information on their local beaches, lakes, and rivers. As well as any other location they wish to visit across the United States.

Waterly allows users to check locations for information regarding health risks they may face while venturing out in areas with advisories that are disrupting water quality, as well as air quality. We also provide information on lifeguard statuses, parking, restrooms, and food and drinks. In order to prevent water pollution, we have a community page where members of our app can register and join local communities in cleaning up.

EDUCATING OUR USERS

Waterly's glossary feature allows users to look up contaminants that we highlight, that they might not be familiar with. This feature not only educates our users but displays images they can go through so they know what to look for and help them identify these contaminants faster. 

MEET UP FOR CLEAN UP

Our community of users can create events that allow them to gather together for clean up initiatives at local beaches, rivers, and lakes. This enables our users to get a word out about their efforts in cleaning up polluted areas.

FINDING LOCAL BEACHES, LAKES, & RIVERS

Users who are visiting warmer areas and want to spend their day enjoying the water can search up any place within the U.S. This will provide them with information to water quality information on their local beaches, lakes, and rivers. As well as, photos of the area, parking, restrooms, lifeguards, and places for food.

UX Process

Research

My first step to designing a solution, was to understand the problem that I was trying to solve. To do that, I needed to figure out how I would retrieve the necessary information and data to alert our users that there were contaminants in the waters. Look into the existing solutions that were already on the market and understand who our primary users are, their goals, pain points, and needs.

Market Research

Through my market research, I uncovered some of the major causes of water pollution and how it or if it also effects air quality and what steps people were taking to help reduce pollution. I also looked into the current solutions that existed on the market. With this research, it gave me a better understanding of who our user might be and the kinds of questions to ask them.

Competitive Analysis

To help understand where Waterly could position itself on the market, I looked into current applications out there. I began with the ones that alert people about water quality at beaches, rivers, and lakes. I looked at how the information was presented and relayed to the users and then looked into apps that promote groups getting together to help combat water pollution. To further understand where these applications receive their information, I reached out to an environmental scientist that works for the New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection, who explained the process she goes through when receiving, verifying, and releasing the data to the necessary places.

With the information I received from the environmental scientist and the data I collected while researching other applications, I realized that as a user, I had a hard time understanding what most of the information that was given to me, meant. There were few products out there that educated users as well as alerted them of conditions. I analyzed their key features on retrieval of information and how they displayed it to their users.

User Interviews

I conducted interviews with people to help myself learn and understand more about the primary user. I outlined questions to prepare for the interviews and gave them a brief description on what the project was about. After the interviews, I transcribed them and arranged the information in an order of importance based off of the similarity of information that came up between the people.

Reflection

Being able to use a medium of choice for this project, it allowed me to continue practicing my design skills in a field I was extremely interested in. This was the first time I created an end-to-end mobile application and I learned a lot about myself as a designer and the steps that needed to be taken in order to create a successful design. An important lesson I learned was 'less is more'. While it's easy to continue adding to designs, it can also overwhelm the design and the users, creating frustrations that could ultimately lose you users. Another important takeaway from this project was understanding my user and the extensive research I had to conduct in order to find my target audience. It allowed me to formulate questions to get a better idea of what their needs and pain points were. When asked why something was done a certain way, I can go back to the research that was conducted and defend the decisions that were made.
